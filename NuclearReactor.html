Nuclear Reactor

Intro

Nuclear reactor the power supply for nuclear power plants, system to produce radioactive isotopes. A device capable of controlling and initiating nuclear fissions. The process when a heavy atomic nucleus is separated into two smaller components. When this happens the smaller components release neutrons which release more neutrons, which cause more fissions. So, a chain reaction has been created and this releases a large amount of energy.The design to increase the intensity until much of the material has fissioned is the design for an atomic bomb.

This sort of expansion is rapid and creates the characteristics of such bombs. whereas the nuclear reactor is controlled and cannot be used as an atomic bomb. Within short time after the fission most of the energy is released, the remaining energy is produced by the fragment of the fission. An energy that must be dealt with the proper reactor design is the radioactive decay which is the process when the atom reaches a more stable state.

Chain reaction a criticality

When the number of neutrons decreases we say that the reactor is in a critical state, whereas when the number of neutrons rises we say that the reactor is in super critical state. When the number of neutrons drops to zero we say that the reactor is in a subcritical state. The deduction of the chain reaction is done by the probability that the neutron will do a fission before the real fission. When a reactor is started up the operators remove control rods until it approaches nominal power level, then they put the rods back in partially balancing the population. In the end when they want to shut down the reactor put the rods in completely.


Reactor control

In order to control the reactor a parameter is used. That parameter is called reactivity and it measures the state of the reactor in relation to the critical state. At subcritical reactivity is negative, at criticality is zero, and at supercritical is positive. There are some ways to control reactivity on is by adding or removing the amount of the absorber, another one is by changing the amount of fuel, and last by changing the ratio of neutrons. The changing of the ratio of neutrons can be done automatically by increasing the power which releases more neutrons and lowers the reactivity, in the other hand the absorbers amount can only be changed by the operators. The derivation of the neutrons and some other processes are crucial to designing a reactor.


Core

Core is the central part of the rector that contains fuel, and where the fission is done. When the fuel contains elements that have fissile material and a diluent we say that it is a heterogeneous fuel. A diluent is an agent with good chemical and mechanical properties and does not absorb neutrons. Diluents act as an environment where the fissile material can stably reside. To prevent or at least minimize the probability of releasing fission elements the material is enclosed in a cladding substance.

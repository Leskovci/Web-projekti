Intro

The force of pull that acts between all matter is called gravity. It does not have a part in defining the properties of everyday matter since it is the weakest force. Even though it has an universal action controlling trajectories of planets and enabling objects to stay attached to ground. Every object on earth has a weight and the gravity force is proportional to their weight. In fact gravity is measured by the speed that it gives to falling objects. The gravity on earth is 9.81 meters per second. Therefore, after each second of falling the gravity will increase for 9.81. Compared to that in the moon the gravitational force is 1.6 meters per second. Two theories that stand out are the ones said by Isac Newton and Albert Einstein. Their theories have implications in continuous researches and have led to great developments.

Early concepts

Aristotle argued that every celestial body had their own natural flow it was not affected by an outside force; in the other hand, Newton argued that gravity was the force which was moving all the celestial bodies. Aristotle’s idea and the ideas that objects needed to have continuous force acting and that it had to be done by contact and not from a distance roamed for quite some time. After some time Johannes Kepler supported Nicolaus Copernicus idea that the planets orbited around the sun and not around the earth. Kepler using geometric and arithmetic equations explained the planetary orbit, and made three quantitative laws.

Electromagnetic Induction

Intro Faradays law induction

Intro

A magnetizing force and magnetic field are established when current passes through a straight conductor. North and South pole are established when the wire is attached into a coil, and the magnetic field is increased which produces a static magnetic field.

The magnetic flux is proportional to the amount current flowing. In order to, strengthen the static magnetic field an extra layer of wires is added. The more wires there are within a coil the stronger the magnetic field gets. We can do the opposite of this. Instead of using electricity to get a magnetic field we will use a magnetic field to get produce electricity. By putting a bar magnet inside the coil and moving it in and out a current will be created.

This can be done in two ways. The first one is by moving the magnet inside the coil, and the second one is by moving the coil while the magnet is stationary. This process is called electromagnetic induction and is one of the basic principles for transformers, generators and motors. Michael Faraday discovered the electromagnetic induction during 1830’s. He noticed that a voltage was produced with the movement of a magnet inside the coil.

In cases when the magnet is removed from the coil from the other side, the galvanometer pointer will go the opposite direction. The same rules apply for the cases when we move the coil instead of the magnet. For Faradays law to be true the magnet, or the coil has to move, or it can be both of them.

Faradays law induction

“ a voltage is induced in a circuit whenever relative motion exists between a conductor and a magnetic field and that the magnitude of this voltage is proportional to the rate of change of the flux”. This is what the law of faraday states. Three different factors can determine how much voltage can be induced. 1) The number of wires if increased can induce more voltage. The more loops that it has the more voltage will be induced. For example if there are twenty loops there will be twenty times more voltage. 2) The faster motion of either the magnet or the coin leads to more voltage, even if the same wire moved through the same field it would depend on the speed. 3) In cases when the speed is the same, but the field is stronger then more voltage will be produced.
